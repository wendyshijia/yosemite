Flying through the Valley

Wendy Shijia Wang

In May 2024, I stood in Yosemite Valley, surrounded by towering granite walls. While the views were breathtaking, something felt incomplete—standing within the embrace of the mountains, I couldn't grasp their entirety.I wish I could glide from El Capitan to Half Dome, seeing the valley a bird might, with the freedom of flight.

This became the seed for this project—a series of four data-driven video artworks. Using GIS and 3D modeling tools, I transformed precise geographical data into immersive visual journeys, merging scientific accuracy with artistic expression, inspired by the memories of my time in Yosemite.

from El Capitan to Half Dome, 35-second

The centerpiece of this collection is a sweeping traverse from El Capitan to Half Dome, deliberately paced to allow viewers to absorb the relationships between Yosemite's iconic formations.

This main sequence is complemented by three focused scenes, each offering a closer, more intimate exploration of the valley’s wonders.

Through a careful blend of art styles, colors, spacing, and timing, the series celebrates the natural beauty of Yosemite AND invites the audiences to experience the landscape with the freedom of imagination.

    Technical Implementation:
  • Data accuracy: Real-world topographical data from USGS, OSM ensures fidelity to Yosemite's geography.
  • Visual references: Colors and textures are derived from Google Maps and on-site photographs and sketches.
  • Digital craftsmanship: Modern tools like CloudCompare, QGIS, and Blender were employed to process the geographical data and reconstruct the landscape in 3D.
